<h2>Places to visit in Seville</h2>
<div class="row">
   <div class="col-sm-4">

      <p class="figure-caption text-end">Plaza de España </p>
      <p>This plaza located in the Maria Luisa square is a must visit landmark in Seville, the plaza is surrounded by buildings that were designed for the Ibero American exhibition of 1929. There are also 52 benhces and mosaics of tile located throughout the square, this creates an amazing surrounding that reflects the traditional and reginal styles. 
</p>

      <img src="images/image1.jpeg" alt="Catedral de Sevilla" style="width:100%">
      <p class="figure-caption text-end">Catedral de Sevilla </p>
 <p>The Seville Cathedral is one of the largest in europe and is registered with UNESCO as a world herritage site. This Cathedral is pefect for gothic architecture lovers or for anyone who visits Seville. It is so grand that it will take you quite some time to catch a glimpse of it's art. It is recommended to join a tour to know more about it's history.</p>  
   </div>
    <div class="col-sm-4">
      <img src="images/IMG-2697.jpg" alt="Las setas de Sevilla" style="width:100%">
      <p class="figure-caption text-end">Las setas de Sevilla </p>
    <p>Las setas de Sevilla is a perfect definition of modern architecture. It is a wooden structure that is located at La Encarnacion square and pretty much covers all ov it. It is considered to be the largest wooden structure in the world, so it's definitely a must visit. During the night time there are blue lights under the structure making it an amazing landmark to appreciate and take pictures of, there are bars nearby that offer amazing tapas and drinks. </p>
